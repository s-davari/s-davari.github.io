About Me

I'm a UX and HCI researcher specializing in intelligent and adaptive XR (AR/VR) systems. My work bridges AI and ML with 3D interaction design and user-centered methodologies to develop context-aware technologies that enhance how we interact with digital content, intelligent agents, virtual environments and avatars, and each other. I integrate machine learning, computer vision, and mixed-methods UX evaluation to inform the design of adaptive interfaces, particularly aimed to ensure productivity, situational awareness, accessibility, social presence and interaction in dynamic, and socially sensitive contexts.

Currently, I'm a Postdoctoral Researcher at Georgia Techâ€™s Symbiotic and Augmented Intelligence Lab (SAIL), where I investigate the design and development of intelligent XR systems for real-world use cases in accessibility and industrial applications. I focus on creating immersive systems that interpret social and physical contexts to adapt in real time, enabling seamless interaction, situational awareness, and meaningful collaboration with both virtual agents and other people.

Previously, I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the 3D Interaction Group at Virginia Tech, where I developed design frameworks and evaluation methodologies for intelligent Augmented and eXtended Reality (AR/XR). As a research intern, I've also collaborated with industry research labs such as Adobe and Microsoft Research, contributing to assistive VR tools and virtual monitors for accessibility, and adaptive spatial interfaces for productivity and avatar-based interaction.

I'm passionate about building systems that are not only intelligent, but also intuitive, inclusive, and responsive to real-world human needs.
