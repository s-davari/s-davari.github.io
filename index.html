About Me

Hi!
I'm Shakiba, a Computer Science Ph.D. student at the 3D Interaction Group at Virginia Tech under the supervision of Doug Bowman. My research interest lies in connecting my past research experience in applied machine learning (ML) and my current passion for AR interfaces. I am a 3D interaction and interface designer, focusing on intelligent AR interfaces. I concentrate on different aspects of detecting AR user's context and utilizing it to adapt their AR interface. Such context-aware interfaces mitigate the existing challenges of AR, such as real-world occlusion and social intrusiveness, while providing more reliable and efficient information access and interaction in AR.

In summer 2022, as a Research Scientist Intern at Adobe, I worked with Jane Hoffswell and Stefano Petrangeli to explore Context-aware document consumption in AR.

I summer 2021, I joined Microsoft as a Research Intern. I enjoyed working with and learning from amazing researchers such as Andrew Begel, Ed Cutrell, Rich Stoakley, and Carmen Badea to explore the applications and benefits of vitual monitors for low-vision users.
